# Scenario: Kakao Open Chat policy backlash

## Purpose
This seed document is for simulating how a leaked policy draft, an official announcement, community backlash, and a follow-up clarification compete over the first **48 hours**.

## Time anchor
- Anchor time: **March 15, 2026, 19:30 KST**
- Observation window: from leak to 48 hours after the official announcement

## Assumed scenario
Kakao plans to announce an Open Chat safety policy that combines **stronger identity checks, 90-day message retention, and tighter limits on some anonymous group operations**.

## Real-world entities referenced
- Kakao
- KakaoTalk / Open Chat
- Korea Communications Commission
- Personal Information Protection Commission
- DC Inside
- Blind
- Naver Cafe
- creators and room operators who rely on Open Chat

## Explicit hypothetical additions
- A screenshot of the draft leaks 6 hours before the official announcement.
- Kakao emphasizes crime prevention, while users and operators frame it as surveillance and the collapse of anonymity.
- Some media outlets foreground youth safety and fraud prevention, while online communities foreground censorship and data retention.

## Stakeholders
- platform/policy: Kakao PR, policy, legal, regulators
- users: room operators, hobby communities, anonymous worker groups, general users
- narrative channels: DC Inside, Blind, Naver Cafe, X, tech YouTubers, reporters
- business: advertisers, creators, commerce operators

## Questions to answer
1. Which community reacts first and most aggressively?
2. Does “safety” or “privacy violation” dominate?
3. What grows faster: churn threats, migration to alternatives, or political framing?
4. Which sentence in Kakao’s follow-up clarification calms users, and which sentence makes things worse?
