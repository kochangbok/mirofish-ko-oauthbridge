# Scenario: Tesla Korea recall reaction

## Purpose
This quick-start seed file is for observing how **brand trust, owner-community defense, competitor-beneficiary narratives, and regulatory pressure** collide after a recall announcement.

## Time anchor
- Anchor time: **March 15, 2026, 18:00 KST**
- Observation window: 72 hours after the announcement

## Assumed scenario
Tesla Korea announces a large recall for some Model Y and Model 3 vehicles due to a possible **battery temperature sensor fault**.

## Real-world entities referenced
- Tesla
- Tesla Korea
- Korea Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport
- Tesla Owners Club Korea
- Bobaedream
- Hyundai Motor
- Kia
- EV-focused YouTube channels

## Explicit hypothetical additions
- During the prior 24 hours, short clips that appear to show smoke from parked cars spread on X and Reddit.
- Tesla calls the recall “preventive,” while some users frame it as a delayed admission of a known issue.
- rival-brand communities aggressively attach an EV quality-comparison narrative.

## Core tensions
- Do Tesla owners frame the recall as proof of failure or as responsible preemption?
- Does the regulator’s statement carry more trust than Tesla’s explanation?
- Do Hyundai and Kia pick up a real beneficiary narrative?
- Do investor narratives and consumer narratives diverge over the first 72 hours?
