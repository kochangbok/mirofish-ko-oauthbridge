# Scenario: Strait of Hormuz tension and the Korean market on Monday open

## Purpose
This is a quick-start seed file for simulating how Korean investor communities, policy interpreters, and sector narratives react **before and after Monday market open**.

## Time anchor
- Anchor time: **Sunday, March 15, 2026, 21:00 KST**
- Observation window: **Monday, March 16, 2026, 07:00~15:30 KST**

## Assumed scenario
U.S. President Donald Trump publicly asks South Korea to provide naval support related to the Strait of Hormuz, and the South Korean government announces a **limited acceptance posture** on Sunday night. The statement is deliberately vague on deployment size and legal process.

## Real-world entities referenced
- Donald Trump
- The South Korean presidential office / Ministry of Foreign Affairs / Ministry of National Defense / Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy
- Bank of Korea
- Samsung Electronics
- SK hynix

- HMM

- SK Innovation

## Explicit hypothetical additions
- Reuters, Yonhap, Korea Economic Daily, and Maeil Business frame the same announcement with slightly different tones.
- After 22:00 KST on Sunday, Korean retail-investor communities begin circulating “defense / shipbuilding / refining” beneficiary lists.
- At the same time, airlines, semiconductors, and petrochemicals start absorbing “oil / FX / foreign futures selling” concern.

## Stakeholders that should exist in the simulation
- policy actors: presidential office, foreign ministry, defense ministry, industry ministry, finance ministry, Bank of Korea
- market actors: foreign index-futures traders, local institutions, retail investors, FX desks, macro strategists
- industry actors: defense, shipbuilding, shipping, airlines, refiners, petrochemicals, semiconductors
- narrative channels: X, Naver stock boards, DC Inside stock communities, Blind, YouTube market channels

## Key transmission channels
1. Hormuz tension -> expected oil spike
2. oil spike -> KRW weakness and inflation fear
3. FX/risk-off -> multiple compression in KOSPI heavyweights
4. geopolitics -> short-term defense / shipbuilding / shipping upside narrative
5. expensive energy -> pressure on airlines / petrochemicals / consumption
