# Historical patterns and interpretation cues

## Patterns worth borrowing
- In auto recalls, trust is often determined more by posture, compensation, and response speed than by the raw defect.
- If social clips spread before the official statement, the emotional imagery often outlasts the technical explanation.
- Tesla-related controversies are often amplified both by brand loyalists and anti-brand communities.

## Lessons for this simulation
1. technical explanation is slow; emotional narrative is fast
2. owners and non-owners can read the same announcement in opposite ways
3. strong apology and compensation can recover some trust, but a “late response” perception can persist
