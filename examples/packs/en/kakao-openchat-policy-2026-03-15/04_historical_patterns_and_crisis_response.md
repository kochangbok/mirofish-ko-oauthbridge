# Historical patterns and crisis-response cues

## Patterns worth borrowing
- Korean platform controversies often follow: internal leak -> community summary -> headline compression -> late official clarification.
- Privacy and anonymity controversies last longer than ordinary feature changes.
- If the apology is late or vague, corporate attitude can become a larger issue than the policy itself.

## Lessons for this simulation
1. the first six hours are usually dominated by community-translated text, not the full policy
2. even if Kakao says “protect,” users hear “retain” and “possible access”
3. the strongest crisis response is usually precise scope control, exceptions, and deletion/access clarity rather than broad moral language
