# Policy background and platform risk

## Why this becomes large
- Open Chat is not just a feature; it is community infrastructure.
- That means policy changes spill into identity, relationship networks, moderation burden, and content continuity.
- Even if framed as safety, identity verification and retention are easily translated into surveillance risk.

## Interpretation guide
1. leaked screenshots usually spread the most inflammatory lines first
2. formal policy text can intensify fear if it sounds legalistic and vague
3. users care less about abstract policy goals than whether their existing rooms become harder to run
4. operators and casual users may react at very different intensity levels

## Key sentences the simulation should absorb
- In a platform crisis, the community summary often becomes more powerful than the official document.
- “Safety” is the PR frame; users shift quickly to “what data is being stored, for how long, and who can access it?”
- Migration narratives create distrust before they create actual churn.
