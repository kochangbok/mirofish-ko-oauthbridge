# Scenario memo
**Topic:** How Korean online communities, media, regulators, and users react when Kakao tightens Open Chat identity and message-retention rules in the name of safety
**Anchor:** 2026-03-15 19:30 KST

## 1. Separate context from injected assumptions
### Real-world context
- Open Chat is used as infrastructure for hobby groups, fandoms, trading, anonymous worker communities, and local gatherings.
- In Korean platform crises, leaked screenshots and community reinterpretation often matter more than the formal policy text.
- Privacy and anonymity concerns create stronger backlash than ordinary UX friction.

### Injected simulation assumptions
- At 18:00 KST on March 15, 2026, part of an internal policy draft leaks to online communities.
- At 23:00 KST, Kakao formally announces an Open Chat safety policy that includes:
  1. stronger identity verification for room operators
  2. a 90-day message-retention period
  3. tighter limits on some large anonymous rooms considered high-risk
- The announcement strongly emphasizes crime prevention but remains vague on retention scope and access procedure.

## 2. Frames that must exist
### Frame A — safety / crime prevention
- the platform must reduce fraud, exploitation, and abuse
- a large platform needs baseline accountability and traceability

### Frame B — privacy invasion / collapse of anonymity
- the core value of Open Chat is being destroyed
- longer retention feels like surveillance and data stockpiling
- users and operators are being treated like suspects

### Frame C — trust / migration
- Kakao is making another unilateral platform decision
- Discord, Telegram, and Naver Cafe start appearing as alternatives
