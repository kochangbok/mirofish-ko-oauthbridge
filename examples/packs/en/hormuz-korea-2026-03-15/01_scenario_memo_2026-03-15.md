# Scenario memo
**Topic:** How Korean public opinion and the Korean stock market react after Donald Trump publicly pushes for South Korean naval support in the Strait of Hormuz and Seoul signals limited acceptance.
**Anchor:** 2026-03-15 21:00 KST

## 1. Separate real-world context from injected assumptions
### Real-world context
- The Strait of Hormuz is a major chokepoint for energy-importing economies.
- Korean markets often translate geopolitical shocks into FX, foreign-flow, and heavyweight-equity language before anything else.
- Defense, shipbuilding, and shipping are common short-term “beneficiary narrative” sectors in this kind of shock.

### Injected simulation assumptions
- Donald Trump publicly names South Korea and asks for naval support to secure shipping through the Strait of Hormuz.
- At 20:40 KST on Sunday, the South Korean presidential office issues a short statement signaling limited acceptance centered on protecting Korean vessels.
- The statement is vague on combat scope, legal process, and deployment size.

## 2. Main frames likely to dominate before Monday open
### Frame A — alliance / national interest
- rejecting the request would be costly for the alliance
- protecting Korean vessels and energy routes is a national-interest issue
- this is limited protection, not direct war participation

### Frame B — escalation / anti-deployment
- this still looks like participation in a widening Middle East conflict
- Korean defense assets should remain focused on the peninsula
- legal and parliamentary process may become controversial

### Frame C — markets / real economy
- oil, USD/KRW, and foreign futures selling become the fastest fear language
- defense and shipbuilding may overheat while airlines and petrochemicals absorb the direct downside narrative

## 3. Agents that should exist in the simulation
- policy: presidential office, foreign ministry, defense ministry, industry ministry, finance ministry, Bank of Korea, ruling and opposition politicians
- markets: foreign futures/spot investors, local institutions, retail investors, FX desks, commodity traders
- industry: Hanwha Aerospace, LIG Nex1, HD Hyundai Heavy Industries, HMM, Korean Air, S-Oil, SK Innovation, Samsung Electronics, SK hynix
- narrative channels: Yonhap, Korea Economic Daily, Maeil Business, YouTube market channels, Naver boards, DC stock communities, Blind
