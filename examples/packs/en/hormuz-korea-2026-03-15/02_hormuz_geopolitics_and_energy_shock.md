# Hormuz geopolitics and the energy shock channel

## Why Korea is sensitive
- The Strait of Hormuz matters because it is not just about physical supply. It is also about insurance, shipping risk, pricing, and inflation expectations.
- For Korea, the market often reacts before any actual supply disruption by repricing oil-sensitive sectors, KRW risk, and foreign-flow behavior.

## Interpretation guide for the simulation
1. higher oil does not automatically mean a clean “refiner benefit” trade
2. refiners can gap up early and still fade if the market rotates into demand destruction or policy-control narratives
3. airlines and petrochemicals are the most intuitive losers
4. semiconductors and autos may react more to foreign selling and KRW weakness than to energy fundamentals directly

## Sentences the simulation should internalize
- In Korea, Hormuz is not just geopolitics; it is an oil, FX, inflation, and risk-premium story.
- The same policy statement can be read as both “energy-security relief” and “escalation participation.”
- Peak fear may arrive at the open, while relief may arrive through clarifications and headline tone shifts.
