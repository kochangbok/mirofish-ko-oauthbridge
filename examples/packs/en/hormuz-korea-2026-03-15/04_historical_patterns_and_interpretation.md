# Historical patterns and interpretation cues

## Patterns worth borrowing
- When Middle East tension jumps, Korea often shows a mix of KOSPI weakness, KRW softness, defense strength, and airline weakness.
- If early fear becomes excessive, government stabilization language or limited-participation framing can trigger a fast reversal.
- Seoul has incentives to choose a frame that looks cooperative with allies without looking like open-ended war participation.

## What that means for this simulation
1. fear arrives in numbers; relief arrives in sentences
2. sector winners and index weakness can coexist
3. the market may respond more to how the news is translated than to the deployment itself
