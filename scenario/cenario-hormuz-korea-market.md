  # Scenario Memo

  ## Date
  Sunday, March 15, 2026, 20:00 KST

  ## Assumption
  The U.S. requests South Korea to provide naval support related to the Strait of Hormuz.
  The South Korean government accepts the request and announces support before the Korean stock market opens on Monday,
  March 16, 2026.

  ## Confirmed in this simulation
  - U.S. request occurs
  - South Korean government accepts
  - The announcement becomes public before market open

  ## Unknowns
  - Scale of deployment
  - Whether it is symbolic support or active escort/operation
  - Domestic political backlash
  - U.S.-Iran escalation path
  - Oil price jump magnitude
  - FX reaction

  ## Market transmission channels
  - Oil supply anxiety
  - Shipping insurance / logistics disruption
  - KRW weakness risk
  - Defense sector narrative
  - Shipbuilding / naval theme
  - Airline and energy-intensive sectors pressure
  - Risk-off sentiment in KOSPI/KOSDAQ

  ## Stakeholders
  - Retail investors
  - Macro traders
  - Energy analysts
  - Defense-theme traders
  - Airlines / logistics watchers
  - Government / media / community opinion leaders

  What narratives dominate from Sunday night to Monday open, and what sectors or themes get bought or sold first?
